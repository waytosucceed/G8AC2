Types of Prepositions and their Usage


  • Prepositions perform the following two functions in a sentence:
    Link two words or a word and a phrase and
    Denote a relationship between those words or the word and the phrase it is linking.
  • In a sentence, a simple preposition can function as more than one type of preposition depending on its use.
  • When ‘to’ sits before the base/first form/infinitive form of a verb it functions as the infinitive marker and not as a preposition
  • There are 12 types of prepositions based on usage:
    1. Simple Prepositions
    2. Phrasal Prepositions
    3. Prepositional Phrases
    4. Prepositions of Place
    5. Prepositions of Time
    6. Prepositions of Direction
    7. Prepositions of Agency or means
    8. Prepositions of Source/Origin
    9. Prepositions of Possession
    10. Prepositions of Accompaniment
    11. Prepositions of Purpose
    12. Prepositions of Condition
